# Craphics!

A simpleton's computer graphics framework in C.

## C? Did you just get out from a cave?

Kinda yes, as many folks out there do, I might say I love the brutal way to learn something. In fact, writing something in C is, however, not *that* brutal at all, you know, if I were truly intened to hurt myself, I rather would have chosen other truly 'notorious' thugs like assembly. I just want to think about the well-known tension between hardware and software (mostly around the matter of pointer) in the path of building it.  

## Why on earth do you do this?

There are literally ton of graphics frameworks out there, of course, and those state-of-the-art programs, moreover, are extremely sophisticated.

But the here's the point — They're all suddenly become somthing "what just it is", we simply think they **somehow** make those incredible images and effects, and disregard what really happens in the very inside of it. They have, at least I think, become a **opaque** like stuff of Old Greeks or People from Tang dynasty, while it's living and working vibrantly just alongside of our lives. It's utterly ridiculous. All those appratuses that enables all the spectacles of our era is not the kind of thing that we can simply disregard as we've been doing.

But one may argue, "I got the gist, but why should it be about 'Computer Graphics'?". Well, in that case, I would say it is because this area is one of the most appropriate gateway to do the comtemplation which I said above. It's all about visual, this property makes it the most intuitive stuff among many subjects, but at the same time, it's completely convered with all kinds of secrets like every other modern 'science' does.

Furthermore, we've got to think of the fact we implicitly assume "what it is" is "what it seems". All the visual images literally 'render' the most part of our lives. 

So my vulgar answer is this — This foolish endevour is the first doorstep to the attitude of "knowing what we do not".
